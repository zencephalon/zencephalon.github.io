I love holy black Aye, I declare to thee that black is holy For in a hole what do we find but black?

Bountiful black, goes with any color yet remains undiminished Like black may I welcome all people and help them shine Beautiful black, mixes with any color yet remains unchanged Like black may I embrace all people and with them intertwine

Black absorbs all! Not a mirror to your soul It does not reflect U Let black consume U Black always accepts It fills your every crack Stare into the black and U see your own nature whole, unending, boundless, deep

Aye, black, the great mother Who embraced U in the womb? Why, black, of course. U cried when U saw the light

The earth sits in her cradle She gave birth to the sun, thus we call him the son

Black always has space for us We build walls yet we live in her room Everywhere we look we see through her

Absence is her presence, aye I love the great mother we all return to her

By Matthew Bunday. ILUVU.
