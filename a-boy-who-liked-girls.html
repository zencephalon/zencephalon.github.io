Once there was a boy who liked girls. He was bright and curious, and so he caught on early to how wonderful girls were. He couldn't have been more than four or five.

He lived in a like-side town named Excelsior. It was a nice town, and he enjoyed going to the library with his father. But most of all, he enjoyed playing with his friends in the neighborhood.

One of his friends was named Anastasia, and he found her to be beautiful. He only remembers a few moments with her now. When she showed him the movie Anastasia, which she liked for an obvious reason. The time she played dress up for him. Her excitement in as she dressed up. His innocent wonderment at the barest glimpses of skin. The night he was the only boy who showed up to watch the beauty pageant, so he could be with her, and they placed a ribbon on him proclaiming him ‘Ms. Something-he-doesn’t-remember’. He was vaguely aware that he should be embarrassed, but it didn’t matter so long as he got to see her.

Then came the fateful day. He had invited her to a lunch date, of sorts. She came over to his apartment. And they sat together eating tuna sandwiches his mother had served. He felt things were going well, and he was happy. Oh, so happy. But unbeknownst to him, his mother had invited her friend to come take the trampoline, and her friend’s children came along.

By Matthew Bunday. ILUVU.
